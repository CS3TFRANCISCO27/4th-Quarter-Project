Economic and Environmental Impact

Sustainable agriculture is an approach to farming that focuses on producing food in a way that is environmentally friendly, economically viable, and socially responsible. Letâ€™s explore its impacts in two key areas: economic and environmental.

Economic Impact

Sustainable agriculture supports farm profitability and local economies, ensures fair treatment of workers, supports new generations of farmers, and promotes equitable access to healthy food.

  • Practices like crop rotation, reduced tillage, and agroforestry enhance farm resilience and productivity while reducing the need for costly inputs such as chemical fertilizers and pesticides.

Environmental Impact

Sustainable agriculture protects and enhances natural resources, minimizes pollution, and promotes biodiversity.

  • It involves building healthy soil, managing water efficiently, planting cover crops, and increasing resilience to extreme weather, thereby mitigating the negative impacts of conventional farming on the environment.
